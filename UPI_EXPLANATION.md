# UPI (Unique Personal Identifier) - Explanation

## What is UPI?

**UPI** stands for **Unique Personal Identifier**. It is a unique identification number assigned to each student in the Kenyan education system.

## Purpose

- **Lifetime Tracking**: UPI follows a student throughout their entire educational journey (from primary to secondary and beyond)
- **National Database**: Used to track students across different schools and education levels
- **Ministry of Education**: Required for official records and reporting to the Ministry of Education
- **Transfer Tracking**: When a student transfers schools, the UPI remains the same, making it easy to track their academic history

## Format

UPI typically follows a format like:
- `UPI2024ABCD1234` (Year + Random alphanumeric)
- Or similar format as specified by the Ministry of Education

## In This System

The system automatically generates a UPI when a student is admitted:
- Format: `UPI[YEAR][8 random characters]`
- Example: `UPI2024A1B2C3D4`
- Unique for each student
- Cannot be changed once assigned

## Difference from Admission Number

- **Admission Number**: School-specific, sequential number (e.g., 100, 101, 102)
- **UPI**: National identifier that follows the student across all schools

## Important Notes

- UPI is mandatory for all students in Kenya
- It must be unique across the entire education system
- Used for official reporting to the Ministry of Education
- Required for CBC (Competency-Based Curriculum) compliance
